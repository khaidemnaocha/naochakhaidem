HISTORICAL NOTE

--------------------

The study of trigonometric was first started in India.
The ancient Indian Mathematicians.Aryabatta(476A.D.),Brahmagupta (598 A.D.),Bhaskar I
(600 A.D.) and Bhaskara II (1114 A.D.) got important results of trigonometry.All this knowledge went from India to Arabia and then from there to Europe.The
Greeks had also started the study of trigonometry but their approach was so
clumsy that when the Indian approach become known,it was immediately adopted
throughout the world.

In Indian, the predecessor of the modern trigonometric functuions,known as
the sine of an angle, and the introduction of the sine function represents one of
the main contribution of the siddhantas(Sanskrit astronomical works ) to mathematics.

Bhaskara I(about 600A.D.) gave formulae to find the values of sine functions
for angles more than 900. A sixteen century Malayalam work Yuktibhasa
contains a proof for the expanssion of sin(A+B).Exact expression for the sines or
coines of 18o,540,720,etc., were given by Bhaskara II
.
